Search Roles

Surveillance of the River

Our river is watched 24 hours a day by a team of CCTV operators looking for vulnerable people that may need help alongside Foyle search and rescue who will through experience know when to watch and search at safe and worthwhile times so the best energy is used in finding your loved one.

More

First Contact

The PSNI Police , Family Liaison Officer (FLO) will contact you in the first instance they will give you the contact details of the WHSCT Family Liaison person who will offer you support at this distressing time.

The FLO will discuss with you there are agreed interagency protocols for addressing the issue of a missing person on the river;

  • That the search will be led by PSNI and supported by FSR. If other agencies were required to assist in the actual search operation they would be directly appointed by PSNI;
  • There will be a follow up visit within 8 hours and that the FLO would be accompanied by a representative from Foyle Search and Rescue – with your consent.
  • AN information and support leaflet will be give to you.
More

Family Support

It is critical that you have clear communication and contact information, to support you through the whole search. Your Family Liaison Officer they will give you the contact details of the WHSCT Family Liaison person who will offer you support at this distressing time.

Your PSNI Family Liaison Officer will chat with you about developments and help reduce the risk of possible things that can effect the search or put other individuals at risk.

More

Vulnerable People Support

If anyone is in immediate crisis use the Lifeline Crisis Service 0808 808 8000 (free to call 24/7). Also share this number with other family members and close friends of your loved on if you think they are also vulnerable at this time.

Alert your flow officer if you feel someone connected may also be venerable and they can guide them to support.

It is normal that you and other’s connected with the missing loved one would experience emotional pain and demonstrate signs of distress.

However, it is important to check with your family and friends how they are feeling. In particular, if they are withdrawn, demonstrating extreme sadness, hopelessness, express negative feelings about themselves - if your concerned read more here
